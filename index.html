Theory

Whirling Speed

The whirling speed of a shaft is the speed at which the rotating shaft starts vibrating laterally with large amplitude due to resonance. This condition occurs when the running speed of the shaft becomes equal to its natural frequency.

NOTE: Whirling speed is equal to the natural frequency speed, and this is also called the critical speed.

Natural Frequency

The natural frequency is the frequency at which the shaft vibrates freely when disturbed.

ωn = √(k / m),    k = 48EI / L3    ⟹    ωn = √(48EI / ρAL4)

Amplitude (Damped Condition)

The amplitude in damped whirling depends on the speed ratio, eccentricity and damping ratio.

X = e(ω/ωn)2 / √[(1 - (ω/ωn)2)2 + (2ζω/ωn)2]

Here, ζ is the damping ratio.
